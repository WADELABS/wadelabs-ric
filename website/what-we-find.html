WHAT WE FIND

Scanners find vulnerabilities. RIC finds invariant breaks — the gaps between secure code and a circumstance-driven user.

SESSION INTEGRITY FAILURES

  • > Logout does not revoke server-side authorization
  • > Stale elevated sessions persist
  • > Step-up missing for sensitive changes
  • > Recovery flows that bypass re-authentication

WORKFLOW DOUBLE-EXECUTION

  • > Multi-tab / double-submit applies actions twice
  • > Retries cause duplicated side effects
  • > Webhook replay vulnerabilities
  • > Non-idempotent “confirm” endpoints

AUTHORIZATION DRIFT

  • > Role boundaries regress after refactors
  • > Cross-tenant object exposure
  • > Admin features reachable from user context
  • > Cache contamination across roles/tenants

EXPORT & ARTIFACT LEAKAGE

  • > Predictable export URLs
  • > Download artifacts leaking across users
  • > Improper authorization on background jobs
  • > Incomplete redaction / stale cache data

BILLING & ENTITLEMENT DRIFT

Some of the most expensive integrity failures are entitlement mismatches: access that persists after cancel, refunds that don’t revoke value, and subscriptions that desynchronize across services.

  • > Cancel without access revocation
  • > Refund without entitlement rollback
  • > Plan upgrades that skip enforcement gates

PRESSURE VARIANTS (PRO)

Integrity failures often appear only under retries, latency, degraded dependencies, and real incident conditions. Pro runs stress variants to surface drift that “works fine” in normal paths.
